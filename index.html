FEIT Festival OF Languages

When: February, 20th 2026
Where: Forum 1, Melbourne Connect

න About

What is it all about?     FEIT is not only the oldest engineering and information technology faculty in Australia but it also features a large community of over 3,000 students supported by around 400 faculty members, representing over 60 countries. Festival of Languages provides an opportunity for faculty members (both students and staff) to meet colleagues and peers and celebrate this great diversity of languages and cultures within the faculty. We kindly invite community members to share their native languages with others (and discuss some fascinating aspects of their cultures!) by giving 10–15 minute presentations. As the event will be hosted within FEIT, we also encourage FEIT students and staff to discuss the state of technology (machine translation, web search, spell checking tools, ASR, etc.) in their native languages.

What will be happening on the day of the event?     Festival of Languages is a day-long (9am to 5pm) event split into three sections, or parts: (1) language presentations; (2) language technology presentations; (3) language trivia games. We will run coffee-breaks between the parts for further networking. The schedule will be published in late Janurary.


🌱 This event is supported by the FEIT Diversity and Inclusion Grant.

ლ Call For Proposals

We invite FEIT students and staff to deliver 10-15 minute presentations about their native languages and cultures. The presentation may include: general information about the language, the status of the language, the number of speakers, some fascinating facts or properties, the state of technology.

No need to be a linguist/scientist, the most important thing is your willingness to share your language.

In order to become a speaker, please submit the form below by January, 20th.

If you have any questions, please contact Ekaterina Vylomova (the main organiser) via vylomovae@unimelb.edu.au
